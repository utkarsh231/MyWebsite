AUTCD-Net: Automated Framework for Efficient Covid-19 Diagnosis on Computed Tomography Scans

Accepted, to be published on
Springer Nature in Lecture Notes in Networks and Systems (LNNS Series; Scopus Indexed)

 The rapid spread of the new pandemic, i.e., COVID-19, has
severely threatened global health. Deep-learning-based computer-aided
screening, e.g., COVID-19 infected CT area segmentation, has attracted
much attention. Therefore, to aid in computer-aided diagnoses and the
complex process of segmenting covid-19 affected regions in a chest CT,
an efficient segmentation method can clinically assist in treating patients
through the location of potential areas of interest. This paper presents
AUTCD-Net, based on hierarchical resolution steps, to efficiently segment Covid-19 infected lung regions in CT scans. This approach results in significant improvement in terms of dice coefficient with respect to the state-of-the-art architectures. Our comprehensive evaluation,
model analysis, and antique insights can be extended to developing the
Computer-Aided Diagnostic (CAD) tool to assist the CT image reading
process for the detection of Covid-19 infected regions in the near future.

  • Date: Aug - Dec 2021

  • Authors: 
    Palash Ghosal, Amish Kumar, Soumya Snigdha Kundu, Utkarsh Prakash Srivastava, Ashis Datta, Dr.HKD Sarma


Face Recognition Based Attendance System

Group Project

In this fast-paced world time is everything. People get more paid for doing work in less time. So, here our project is for saving time for both students and teachers. The traditional attendance system relies on paperwork. This means teachers must enter all the data of the students back from the paper to a computer, or worse create some kind of paper filing system. The potential for errors is significant and the time it takes to manually organize the data can be excessive.

With the online attendance system, the student marks their attendance via an online session. When the students mark their attendance through online sessions, it is automatically organized and stored in an online database that the teacher can immediately access anywhere, anytime. This system is designed using HTML, CSS, JAVASCRIPT, Django, SQLite, and Python. The front-end section is designed using HTML, CSS, and JAVASCRIPT, and the back-end section is designed using Django and the database using PostgreSQL. For the recognition system, we used 
used the ‘Histogram of Oriented Gradients (HOG)’ as the algorithm of the model. This system software is more secure, user-friendly, and less time-consuming. 

  • Date: Dec 2021 – Jun 2022
  • Team Members :  Utkarsh Prakash Srivastava, Keshabh Kedia and Sahil Agarwal
